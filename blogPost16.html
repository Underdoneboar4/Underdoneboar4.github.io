Belief. The first section of the reading talks a lot about “religious arguments”, where teams get stuck on basic features due to members’ own personal biases and preferences. What a designer wants is often very different from what a developer wants which is also often very different from what a manager wants. All this disagreement stems from different beliefs and values belonging to members of the various groups. However, when designing an effective website, it’s a good idea to shift the focus of “do all our users want X” to “is X the right choice in this scenario?”. There isn’t the same conviction behind it as “will our users love us doing X on our website” but it gets the job done. This will be helpful in my career because Designing things on teams with diverse perspectives is a huge part of basically every software development role. https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/don-t-make-me-think-key-learning-points-for-ux-design-for-the-web
