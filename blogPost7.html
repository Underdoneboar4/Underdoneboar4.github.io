Confusion. The DMMT section of the reading is focused on webpage layout design, which is initially compared to walking around a large department store. At the end of the reading, the Trunk Test is used to show good lower-level webpage design. Those sections and everything in-between are about minimizing confusion for users. If someone doesn’t know where they are or where they need to go, then the website is poorly designed. This is important for me to remember in my career because good websites are good products, and a confusing website is a bad website.
