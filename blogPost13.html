Conflict. Designing a presentation as art conflicts with many other areas of typical business settings. Rules, regulations, stuffy suits, they all clash with designing something that is artsy and creative. “Throwing a template” at a presentation as a solution could be okay, but it likely won’t cover the nuances of the topic you want to talk about, even if it makes more sense from a “time is money” state of mind. Slowing down, finding solitude, and meditative behaviors can help get the creative juices flowing, but it may not “feel right” from a corporate perspective. This is important for me to remember in my career because slowing down and treating presentations like a creative process can help make them more impactful without much cost to efficiency.
